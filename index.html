About

Architecture meets art psychotherapy.

Visible Women, Visible Spaces combines architecture, feminist urban research, participatory methods and therapeutic creative practice to explore how women emotionally and spatially experience public space.

Through workshops, walking conversations, behavioural mapping, storytelling and visual making, women’s lived experiences become part of shaping urban futures.

Dr May Newisar

Architect · Urban Researcher

Researching women’s safety, spatial justice, participatory urbanism and public-space governance through architecture and community engagement.

Thahmina Begum

Artist-Researcher · Art Psychotherapist

Mixed-media artist, workshop facilitator and HCPC registered Art Psychotherapist exploring trauma, wellbeing, heritage and community voice.

Research

Research rooted in women’s lived experiences.

Research combines architecture, behavioural mapping, walking methods, observation and creative engagement to understand how women emotionally and spatially experience public space.


The work explores safety, belonging, visibility, identity and inclusivity within everyday urban life.
